# Readings and Operations

This page explains how operations users verify telemetry quality from the portal.

## Pages to Use

- `/devices`: inspect a device and view recent readings for one selected device.
- `/readings`: filter readings by selected sites/devices and inspect full JSON payloads.
- `/signal-readings`: inspect normalized signal streams.
- `/templates`: verify template, block, and signal structure.

## Recommended Verification Routine

1. Open `/devices` and confirm the target device exists at the expected site.
2. Open `/readings` and filter to that device.
3. Confirm timestamp freshness and payload shape.
4. Open `/signal-readings` to confirm downstream normalized signals.

## Typical Operational Questions

### Why do I see a device but no readings?

- device exists, but ingestion has not started.
- Cloud Bridge payload is missing `data.serialNumber`.
- serial number in payload does not exactly match the device serial.
- ingestion failed due to schema or duplicate policy.

See: [Cloud Bridge Troubleshooting](../cloud-bridge/troubleshooting.mdx)

### Why are readings present but signals missing?

- template/block/signal mapping is incomplete.
- mapping slot ambiguity exists and needs explicit slot selection.
- signal extraction path is incorrect.

See: [Template and Mapping APIs](../api/mapping-and-templates.mdx)

## Operational Safety Notes

- verify organization context before performing write actions.
- use API-key automation for recurring imports; avoid manual repetitive updates in UI for high volume workloads.
